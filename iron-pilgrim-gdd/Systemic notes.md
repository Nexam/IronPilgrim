# Iron Pilgrim — Systemic Design Notes

## Core Design Direction

Iron Pilgrim should focus on:
- Interacting systems
- Emergent situations
- Systemic storytelling

The strongest moments should come from systems colliding together, not scripted events.

Example chain:
- Damaged leg
- Altered gait
- Increased power consumption
- Overheating regulator
- Sensor failure
- Navigation issues
- Forced detour
- Creature encounter

The game should generate stories through simulation and consequence.

---

# Terrain & Traversal

Terrain must affect gameplay mechanically, not only visually.

Terrain influences:
- Footing quality
- Stability
- Heat
- Power consumption
- Noise
- Visibility
- Movement speed
- Balance

Examples:
- Mud
- Ash dunes
- Steep slopes
- Shallow water
- Ruined structures
- Magnetic debris fields
- Creature nests

Goal:
- Procedural locomotion becomes gameplay, not just animation.

---

# Cargo Integrity

The cargo should silently influence player behavior.

The mech prioritizes cargo protection automatically.

Examples:
- Unsafe actions refused
- Power rerouted to cargo stabilization
- Cooling prioritized for cargo
- Sprinting restricted in dangerous terrain
- Violent impacts trigger stabilization mode
- Weapon systems disabled during emergency cargo protection

The player should gradually understand:
> The machine is protecting something important.

This should happen through systems, not exposition.

---

# Repairs & Identity

Repairs are not simple upgrades.

The mech should become:
- Improvised
- Patched together
- Asymmetrical
- Personalized by survival

Salvaged components may:
- Consume more power
- Change locomotion feel
- Alter silhouette
- Affect balance
- Emit different sounds
- Interact differently with factions

The mech evolves physically through repairs.

---

# The Signal

The signal should behave like a systemic force, not just a quest marker.

Possible interactions:
- Storm interference
- Terrain distortion
- Signal amplification in ruins
- Faction jamming
- Sensor triangulation
- Creature reactions
- Memory fragment triggers

As sensors improve:
- Signal clarity improves
- Direction becomes shape
- Shape becomes pattern
- Pattern becomes almost recognizable

The signal should feel physically present in the world.

---

# Combat Philosophy

Combat is:
- Rare
- Dangerous
- Costly
- Undesirable

The mech is not a combat machine.

Combat consequences should matter:
- Structural damage
- Limping
- Power loss
- Heat spikes
- Component failures
- Cargo risk

The optimal solution should often be:
- Avoidance
- Stealth
- Detours
- Hiding
- Environmental traversal

Goal:
- Preserve the game's survival/traversal identity.
- Avoid drifting into a traditional mech action game.

---

# Systemic Interconnection

All major systems should influence each other:
- Terrain
- Locomotion
- Weight
- Heat
- Damage
- Stability
- Power
- Cargo integrity
- Signal reception

Avoid isolated mechanics.

The game becomes strongest when systems constantly create side effects and tradeoffs.
