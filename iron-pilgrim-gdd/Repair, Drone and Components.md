
## Core Gameplay Loop

Primary loop:

1. Walk
2. Scan / scout
3. Salvage
4. Repair / maintain
5. Tune systems
6. Continue toward the signal

Traversal and maintenance are the two core pillars of the game.

---

# Repair Philosophy

Repairs should feel:
- Physical
- Tactile
- Slow and deliberate
- Atmospheric

Avoid:
- Pure abstract RPG inventory management
- Full freeform engineering sandbox / voxel building

Target direction:
- Physical interaction + abstract system management

Reference feeling:
- Space Engineers (maintenance feel)
- Metro Exodus (diegetic maintenance)
- Pacific Drive (vehicle relationship)

---

# Drone

The drone is:
- A mech extension, not a separate character
- Light and nimble
- Short-range
- Used for:
  - 3rd-person camera feed
  - Local scouting
  - Maintenance / repair

The mech remains the center of gameplay.

---

# Drone Gameplay

The drone physically:
- Inspects the mech
- Welds damaged parts
- Replaces components
- Interacts with salvage
- Transfers materials into repairs

Repairs are not voxel building.

Repairs consume resources from mech inventory:
- Metal plates
- Coolant
- CPU parts
- Wiring
- Etc.

---

# Drone Damage

Drone can be:
- Damaged
- Disabled
- Destroyed

When docked on the mech:
- Slowly repairs itself
- Recharges over time

If destroyed:
- The drone is permanently lost

Replacement drones can be found in the world:
- Industrial standard units
- Dormant or damaged
- Must be paired/hacked before use

Possible pairing methods:
- Simple hacking mini-game
- Signal pairing
- Authorization bypass

Replacement drones are imperfect:
- Different condition
- Different quirks
- Different performance
- Possible degraded systems

Goal:
- Avoid hard-locks
- Preserve emotional attachment to the current drone

---

# Components

The mech uses a defined set of components:
- Legs
- Gyro
- Front camera
- Radar
- Cooling system
- Reactor
- Cargo stabilizer
- Etc.

Each component has:
- Health
- Functionality
- Power usage
- Possible overheating/degradation states

Damage should rarely be binary.

Prefer:
- Degraded
- Intermittent
- Unstable
- Overheating
- Power hungry
- Misaligned
- Noisy

---

# Cockpit System Management

Physical repair is done externally with the drone.

System management is done internally from the cockpit.

Cockpit gameplay includes:
- Power allocation
- System rerouting
- Overcharging
- Performance tuning
- Component tweaking
- Heat management

Goal:
- Physical layer = immersion / embodiment
- Cockpit layer = strategy / optimization

---

# Important Design Direction

Systems should interact heavily:
- Locomotion
- Damage
- Heat
- Weight
- Stability
- Power routing
- Cargo protection

Repairs and upgrades should alter:
- Feel
- Movement
- Stability
- Sound
- Silhouette
- Power consumption

The player should feel upgrades through locomotion first, stats second.

---

# Scope Guards

Avoid:
- Full modular mech construction
- Voxel building
- Arbitrary mech assembly
- Drone replacing mech gameplay
- Fast arcade drone gameplay

The mech should maintain:
- Strong identity
- Recognizable silhouette
- Physical presence
- Weight
